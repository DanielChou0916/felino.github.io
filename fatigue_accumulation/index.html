ðŸš€ Fatigue Accumulation

Fatigue describes the accumulation of material degradation under cyclic loading. Notations:

  • simulation time steps: \{1,2,..,t-1,t,t+1,...\}
  • current (instantaneous) energy at time \(t\): \(\Psi_t\)
  • accumulated energy at time \(t\): \(\bar\alpha_t\).
  • energy increment from step \(t-1\) to step \(t\): \(\Delta\bar\alpha_t\).
  • update equation: \(\bar\alpha_t= \bar\alpha_{t-1} + \Delta\bar\alpha_t\).

ðŸ§ª Implementation

1. Regular Mode

Based on conceptual definition, the increment fatigue energy is calculated by comparing current energy at two steps:

\[ \Delta\bar\alpha_t = \begin{cases} \Psi_t - \Psi_{t-1}, & \Psi_t > \Psi_{t-1},\\ 0, & \text{otherwise}. \end{cases} \]

2. Cycle-count-based Linear Accumulation (CLA)

For constant peak loading, instead of energy increment \(\Delta\bar\alpha_t\), accumulated energy is directly calculated by \[ \bar\alpha_t = N_t \times \Psi_{peak} \] The major drawback is that it only handles constant peak load cases.

3. Incremental Cycle-count-based Accumulation (ICLA)

\[ \Delta\bar\alpha_t = \Delta N\times \Psi_t \] where \(Delta N = N_t - N_{t-1}\) is the cyclic count increment.
