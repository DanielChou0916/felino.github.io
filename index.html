An Open-Source Phase-Field Platform for Metallic and Geomaterial Fatigue Analysis

Fatigue failure occurs far more frequently in real-world applications—across metals, rocks, concrete and other geomaterials—than monotonic fracture, yet it remains underrepresented in most computational modeling frameworks. Despite the abundance of ad hoc assumptions in academic theories, their practical validity still requires thorough validation in realistic multiphysics settings. Felino bridges this gap by enabling coupled simulations of phase-field fracture, mechanical equilibrium, and heat conduction—and extending seamlessly from metal fatigue to geomaterial behavior (e.g. rock, soil and concrete). Advanced features include an adaptive cycle-jump algorithm for accelerating high-cycle fatigue analysis and anisotropic material modeling (so-called asymmetric response) for both additively manufactured components and pressure-sensitive geomaterials.

Felino's goal is to provide a unified platform for numerical fatigue and geomaterial analysis, bringing state-of-the-art research tools closer to industry-relevant applications.
