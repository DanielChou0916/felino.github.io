Asymmetric Three Points Bending Fatigue Test

The computational domain measures $100\,\text{mm} \times 5\,\text{mm} \times 20\,\text{mm}$. On the right-hand side (positive $x$ direction), three vertically aligned holes are introduced, extending along the $y$ direction. In the $z$ direction, two support points are placed on the bottom surface—one as a hinge and the other as a roller—while a prescribed vertical displacement $u_{z(\max)} = 0.01\,\text{mm}$ is applied at the center of the top surface. Anisotropy is incorporated by assigning a weak-plane inclination angle $\theta$ in the $x$–$z$ plane. The objectives are:

  • Check the capacity of 3D fatigue test. And provide a standard input file for users.
  • Examinate the functionality of anisotropy director object.
