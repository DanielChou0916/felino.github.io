3D Simulations for Composite Material

This example considers a three-dimensional concrete cylinder with a matrix-inclusion structure. Two types of simulations are performed: a uniaxial compression test and a Brazilian splitting test.

  • To provide a representative example of three-dimensional simulations.
  • To verify that the composite response remains bounded by the strength limits of its constituent materials.

Results and Post-processing



The uniaxial compression stress-strain curve shows the compressive strength of concrete column is approximately 49.32 MPa. From the Brazilian splitting force-displacement curve, the peak loading response is approximately 30,595 in the model-consistent force unit. Using the peak loading quantity extracted from the simulation, the tensile strength is estimated as
\[ \sigma_t = \frac{2P_{max}}{\pi H D} = \frac{2\times 30595}{\pi \times 93.68 \times 50.56} \approx 4.11 MPa \]
This result indicates that even a small inclusion volume fraction (0.062) with higher strength can significantly enhance the overall mechanical response of the composite. Both results are consistent with the expected composite behavior, where the effective strength is bounded between those of the weakest and strongest constituents.
